## 1) The basic claim

RT treats “the world” as something that is **built**, not assumed.

Not built from tiny billiard-ball particles, but from **TimeParticles (TP)** that evolve under a discrete drive and thereby **write spiral-like traces** in a simple arena. Atoms are not primitives — atoms are what you get when multiple TP traces **lock** into stable mutual rhythms.

---

## 2) The arena: PP (Primal Plane)

RT starts from an arena called **PP (Primal Plane)**:

- PP is an **infinite xy-plane**.
- **z is time**. (z is not depth. Space is xy; time is z.)

So PP is not “space-time as a static block”. PP is where form exists; time is the ordering direction.

---

## 3) Where observation lives: RP (Real Plane)

Humans do not observe PP directly. RT uses **RP (Real Plane)**:

- RP is the **readout boundary**: the screen where observables are expressed.
- RP is not the generative arena; it is how structure becomes *measurable*.

Think: **PP generates structure**; **RP expresses what can be read out**.

---

## 4) The engine: TickPulse

Nothing evolves “by itself”. The engine is a discrete pulse:

- **TickPulse** updates the state. The update is **globally synchronous**: each tick advances the **entire PP state** (one universe-step per tick).
- The next state is selected by deterministic constraints (“gates”) rather than by tuning toward external targets.

A useful intuition: TickPulse is not only a clock — it is the mechanism that produces the next moment.

---

## 5) What exists: TimeParticles (TP) and their traces

The primitive “actor” in RT is a **TimeParticle (TP)**:

- A TP is an active, tick-driven entity that evolves under deterministic gates.
- As it evolves, it **writes a trajectory in PP**: typically a spiral-like curve that is the TP’s **trace** through the infinite xy-plane as time (z) advances.
- The curve is not the TP itself; it is the **record** of the TP’s motion/state in PP.

So: **TP is the mover**; **the spiral is what it leaves behind**.

---

## 6) Why two strands (A/B) exist

A single trace is not enough. RT uses an **A/B rule**:

- Every TP comes with two strands related by a strict phase relation.
- This pairing is not optional; it is part of what makes stable identities possible under the gates.

In plain language: A/B is a built-in “self-consistency partner” that prevents many unstable or ambiguous states.

---

## 7) How atoms appear: locking (not forces)

RT does not begin with forces. It begins with **compatibility**.

When multiple TPs share the same PP arena and the same TickPulse, their combined evolution can be:

- **compatible** (the gates can be satisfied simultaneously), or
- **incompatible** (the rules cannot be satisfied at the same time).

Binding happens when TPs find a joint configuration that:
1) passes the gates (symmetry/audit constraints),
2) avoids forbidden overlap,
3) minimizes internal mismatch across couplings (phase tension / inconsistency).

In plain language:
**an atom is a stable rhythm-relationship between TimeParticles.**

Not “a nucleus pulling electrons”, but a set of TPs that remain stable only when they settle into a shared repeating pattern.

---

## 8) From atoms to the world

Once “atom = stable lock” exists, the rest scales:

- molecules = locks between locks,
- materials = long-range compatibility patterns among many locks,
- macroscopic stability = the fact that most configurations are forbidden or unstable, so matter collapses into a small set of robust rhythmic solutions.

The world looks solid because stable locks are rare but persistent.
