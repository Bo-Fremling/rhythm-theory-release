# RT — Lemma pack: Z3, Z6, rho=10, and the (2,−1,−1) weight (Release / v1)

Scope: this note records **structural lemmas** used throughout the current V7 pipeline.
It is written **Core-first** (no SI, no PDG/CODATA, no overlay numbers).

## Definitions (minimal)

- **C30 strobe:** sample indices \(k\in\{0,\dots,29\}\) with angles \(u_k = 2\pi k/30\).
- **A/B (Z2):** two strands related by a \(\pi\)-phase rule (bookkeeping parity channel).
- **Z3 sectors:** a 3-sector ledger on the strobe lattice; the sector label is \(s(k)=k\bmod 3\).

A convenient numerical identity that is used repeatedly:
- A phase shift of \(\pm 2\pi/3\) corresponds to \(\pm 10\) steps on the C30 lattice since
  \(2\pi/3 = 10\cdot(2\pi/30)\).

---

## Lemma 1 — The real Z3 weight is (2, −1, −1)

On a 3-cycle, the discrete Fourier modes are \((1,1,1)\) and \((1,\omega,\omega^2)\), \((1,\omega^2,\omega)\) where \(\omega=e^{2\pi i/3}\).
The unique nontrivial **real** mode is the real part:
\[\Re(1,\omega,\omega^2) = (1, -1/2, -1/2).\]
Scaling by 2 gives **(2, −1, −1)**.

**Uniqueness:** any nontrivial real Z3-symmetric weighting is proportional to \((2,-1,-1)\) up to permutation of sectors.

Interpretation in the pipeline: this is the structural origin of the canonical Z3 weight used as a charge/ledger pattern.

---

## Lemma 2 — Z2×Z3 closes to Z6 (π/3)

Allowed phase offsets from the two gates are generated by
- \(0,\pi\) from A/B, and
- \(0,\pm 2\pi/3\) from Z3.

The additive closure of these offsets is the set of multiples of \(\pi/3\), i.e. a **Z6** quantisation.

Operational consequence: when a lock enumerates phase candidates under A/B and Z3, the candidate phases naturally land on \(\{m\pi/3\}\).

---

## Lemma 3 — C30 makes Z3 “ten-step exact”

Because \(2\pi/3\) is exactly 10 C30 steps, any Z3-sector rotation that is expressed as a phase shift can be represented on the strobe lattice without approximation.

This is a **sampling identity**, not a physical “slot permutation”: it states that the same sampling rule \(u_k\) can represent Z3 offsets exactly.

---

## Lemma 4 — Conditional: why 21-tick packets appear under (cap=7)×(Z3)

Assume the Global Frame setup used elsewhere in the Release:
- \(L_* = 1260\) ticks (C30 closure),
- a cap rule with \(L_{cap}=7\) ticks,
- and an active Z3 sector ledger.

Then a natural composite packet length is
\[L_{pkt} = 3\cdot L_{cap} = 21.\]
Since \(1260/21 = 60\), the global loop accommodates an integer number of such packets.

Status: **conditional lemma**. It is a consistency observation used for organising discrete candidate windows; it does not introduce new Core degrees of freedom.

---

## Lemma 5 — rho=10 is the “Z3-compatible” divisor of C30

Given C30, the divisor structure is fixed. The value
\[\rho=10\]
is the smallest factor that keeps a nontrivial Z3 sectoring aligned with the 30-slot lattice (because \(30/10=3\)).

Status: this is a **compatibility statement** (C30 + Z3 + a ten-per-tick audit), not a standalone postulate.
